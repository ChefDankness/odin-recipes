Spicy Tuna


Description

Tuna poke is a traditional Hawaiian dish made with fresh, cubed raw tuna that’s lightly seasoned and served over rice or greens. The fish is typically marinated in a mixture of soy sauce, sesame oil, and rice vinegar, which enhances its natural flavor without overpowering it. Chopped green onions, sesame seeds, and seaweed are often added for texture and depth. Some variations include ingredients like avocado, cucumber, or mango for a touch of sweetness and freshness. The dish is cool, refreshing, and full of umami, making it both satisfying and healthy. Tuna poke captures the essence of island cuisine—simple, fresh, and bursting with flavor.

Ingredients

  • 8 oz Fresh Tuna
  • 1 Tbl Soy Sauce
  • 1 Tbl Sesame Oil
  • 1 Tbl Sriracha
  • 1/2 tsp Honey
  • 1 Tbl sliced Scallion
  • 1/2 Lemon

  1. Dice tuna into 1/4-1/2 inch chunks. You can go bigger or smaller depending on how you like for it to be.
  2. Combine remaining ingredients except for the lemon into a bowl. Mix to make sure honey is fully incorporated.
  3. Add diced tuna to the bowl and mix. Add juice from the lemon and salt to slowly: taste as you add little by little until its seasoned to your liking.
  4. Can be eaten by itself or served over rice or a salad.
